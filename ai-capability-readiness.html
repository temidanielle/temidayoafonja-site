AI Capability Readiness Diagnostic

Most AI-readiness assessments check whether you have the artifacts of readiness: a policy, a budget, a few pilots in flight. This one measures whether your organization can metabolize hard change at all.

What the engine reads

Fifteen behavioral statements, scored against the last twelve to twenty-four months of your organization's actual life, place you on two axes. Density measures how high-challenge your environment really is: intensity, not tenure. Optionality measures whether the capability you build travels across functions and industries. Alumni Capital, the third pillar, does not move your position. It decides whether the position you hold compounds or leaks when people move and the work changes.

You will land in one of four quadrants: Compounding, Depth Trap, Stagnant, or Fragile. The engine then generates a resilience read in plain language: what your position means for AI absorption, what it is costing you, and what leadership owes the organization next.

Density to Optionality to Alumni Capital produces Institutional Resilience.

This is the self-serve read: one respondent, fifteen core statements, a structured diagnosis in about seven minutes. The full commissioned engagement runs the complete twenty-seven-item instrument across your leadership team, adds evidence review and a leadership interview, and returns an interpreted read from Temidayo Afonja. Scores here are working hypotheses until they are triangulated.

Built by Temidayo Afonja, founder of The Density Group. Eighteen years designing and running capability programs inside Big 4 consulting, regulated life sciences, and enterprise technology. M.S. Cybersecurity Risk and Strategy, NYU. The instrument runs on the Capability Formation Framework, her proprietary model for how organizations build capability that survives disruption.
