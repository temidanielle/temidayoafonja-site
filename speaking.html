Signature Topics

Three arguments the framework is built to make.

01
The Four States of Capability, and Why Most Organizations Are Producing the Wrong One

Why organizations produce Depth Traps and Fragile performers at scale, and what Compounding capability requires that comfort-based development never delivers. The Career Strategy Matrix makes this immediately visual and actionable for senior audiences. Leaves the room with a framework they can use the next morning.

60 to 90 min · Leadership offsites · CHRO peer groups
02
Alumni Capital as Competitive Advantage, and What Leaves With Your People

The organizations winning the next decade are not just retaining talent. They are designing for circulation, building institutional gravity that draws capability back. This talk reframes how leaders think about exits, alumni networks, and the residual value of the people who have already left.

45 to 60 min · Executive retreats · Board sessions
03
Building Capability That Outlasts Volatility, and What Organizations Owe the People Who Build Them

The keynote version of the book's core argument. Why AI adoption without capability density produces cosmetic transformation. What the current wave of restructuring is revealing about how organizations actually developed their people. And what the ones that come out stronger will have built differently before the disruption arrived.

45 to 75 min · Conferences · Company all-hands

For Event Organizers

Speaker biography.

Temidayo Afonja is an organizational capability architect and the creator of the Capability Formation Framework, a diagnostic system for understanding how organizations build, retain, and circulate human capability across Density, Optionality, and Alumni Capital.

Her work draws on seventeen years of practice across Big 4 consulting, global life sciences, and high-growth technology, working with leadership teams facing restructuring, AI transition, and the organizational design challenges that conventional talent strategy was never built to solve.

She speaks to the questions organizations are asking but not yet answering clearly: what it costs to build people well, what it costs not to, and what the architecture of compounding capability actually looks like in practice. Her book, Density: Building Capability That Outlasts Volatility, is in development.
